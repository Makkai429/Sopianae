Sopianae - The Roman town beneath Pécs

Sopianae is one of the most fascinating archaeological and cultural treasures of Central Europe.

Hidden beneath the streets of modern Pécs lies a Roman town that once served as a vibrant administrative, commercial, and religious center on the northern frontier of the Roman Empire. Although nearly two millennia have passed since its founding, Sopianae continues to shape the identity of the city above it. Its remains — painted burial chambers, monumental mausoleums, and fragments of Roman architecture — offer a rare glimpse into the everyday life, beliefs, and artistic traditions of a late Roman provincial community.

We invite you to explore Sopianae in depth. You will learn how the town emerged and developed, what it looked like at its peak, why it became a center of early Christianity, and how its legacy lives on in the cultural life of Pécs today. Whether you are a visitor, a student, or simply curious about the ancient world, Sopianae offers a journey into a past that is still remarkably present.
