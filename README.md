# Sopianae - The Ancient Roman City

# Overview
This website presents an accessible, modern introduction to Sopianae, the ancient Roman predecessor of today’s city of Pécs, Hungary. The project aims to showcase the historical significance of the settlement, its internationally recognized archaeological heritage, and the ways in which its legacy continues to influence the cultural life of Pécs today. The site also includes an interactive JavaScript-based image gallery for visual exploration.
Pages Included

1. Introduction
 
Provides a short, engaging overview of the purpose of the website.
It outlines what visitors can expect to learn, from the origins of Sopianae to the remains still visible in modern Pécs.

2. What Was Sopianae?
   
Explains the historical background of Sopianae as a Roman-era settlement.
Topics include:

Its role within the province of Pannonia
Urban development and daily life in the Roman town
Key archaeological findings and their historical value

3. Why Did Sopianae Become Famous?

Highlights the main reasons Sopianae is known internationally today:

- UNESCO World Heritage designation of the Early Christian Necropolis
  
- The importance of archaeological research conducted in the area

- Preservation efforts and discoveries under Pécs’ modern streets

- Notable Roman ruins still visible in the city, including burial chambers, early Christian tombs, and architectural fragments

4. How Pécs Preserves the Sopianae Tradition

Explores the living heritage of Sopianae in today’s Pécs:

- Festivals and cultural events inspired by ancient traditions
  
- Museums and exhibitions presenting artifacts and historical reconstructions
  
- Public spaces, monuments, and guided tours that connect modern life with Roman history

Purpose of the Project
The goal of this website is to offer an educational, visually appealing resource for students, tourists, and anyone interested in Roman history or local Pécs heritage. It combines historical content with modern web design to make the story of Sopianae both accessible and engaging.
