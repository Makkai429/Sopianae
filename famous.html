Why Sophianae is Famous?

Sopianae is best known for its Early Christian Necropolis, a complex of underground burial chambers and above-ground memorial chapels dating from the 4th century. These structures are remarkable for their architectural innovation and their beautifully preserved frescoes depicting biblical scenes, symbolic motifs, and portraits of the deceased.

UNESCO World Heritage Site

In 2000, the Early Christian Necropolis of Pécs was inscribed on the UNESCO World Heritage List. It is recognized for:

  • Its unique combination of Roman and early Christian architecture
  • The exceptional preservation of its frescoes
  • Its importance in understanding the spread of Christianity in the Roman Empire

Where to Experience Sopianae Today

Most of Sopianae’s visible remains are concentrated around Szent István tér, next to the Pécs Cathedral. Key sites include:

Cella Septichora Visitor Centre

A seven‑apsed burial chapel, unique in the Roman world. The visitor center provides access to multiple underground chambers and offers detailed exhibitions on the history of Sopianae.

Early Christian Mausoleum

One of the largest and most impressive Roman-era structures in Hungary, featuring both an underground crypt and an above-ground chapel.

Painted Burial Chambers

Accessible via modern walkways and glass floors, these chambers display frescoes of:

  • Biblical scenes
  • Christian symbols
  • Decorative patterns
  • Portraits of early believers

Archaeological Exhibitions

Museums and visitor centers in Pécs display artifacts from Sopianae, including pottery, tools, jewelry, coins, and architectural fragments. These objects help reconstruct daily life in the Roman town.
